Hi! I'm a machine learning researcher currently working at Waymo Research. I completed my Ph.D. at UC Berkeley advised by Sergey Levine, and my B.S. (also at Berkeley) advised by Pieter Abbeel.

Broadly, I'm interested in understanding how to engineer machines to solve complex decision making problems. Examples of specific questions I'm interested in exploring include:

  • How can we develop models that are robust to distribution shift, adversarial inputs, and extrapolate to novel data?
  • How can we engineer ML systems that are 99.999% reliable with safety guarantees?
  • How can we design ML that moves away from memorization and pattern recognition and performs better at long-horizon reasoning tasks?
Examples of research areas I study include reinforcement learning, natural language processing, and robotics. For a list of my publications, please see my Google Scholar profile.
